# desde vs desde hace difference?

"Desde" and "desde hace" are both prepositions in Spanish, but they are used in slightly different contexts:

1. **Desde:**
    
    - It is used to express a specific point in time.
    - Example: "Vivo aquí desde enero" (I have been living here since January).
2. **Desde hace:**
    
    - It is used to express the duration of an action or a state that started in the past and continues into the present.
    - Example: "Trabajo aquí desde hace dos años" (I have been working here for two years).

In summary, "desde" is used for a specific point in time, while "desde hace" is used to indicate the duration of an ongoing action or state.
